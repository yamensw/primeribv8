Industries

Retail

Major retailers use AR to reduce returns and increase confidence: preview products at true scale, explore features, and “try” items before buying.

Consumer Tech

Companies like Apple, Amazon, and Best Buy have all leaned into AR experiences—making it normal for customers to interact with products visually, not just via photos.

Social + Discovery

Snapchat and Meta helped normalize AR for nearly a decade. Prime Rig takes that familiar interaction and brings it into business workflows.

Unlike AI-heavy stacks, browser-based AR doesn’t require a large language model or a data-center-sized runtime. The experience streams lightweight 3D assets and runs locally on the customer’s device—fast, private, and scalable.

About

Yamen Seanwan • CEO & Founder

Turning “cool demos” into business-ready AR.

Prime Rig started from a simple problem: businesses had access to incredible AR capabilities, but no practical way to deploy them fast, consistently, and beautifully. I built Prime Rig to productize WebAR—using proven platforms and best practices—so companies can launch showrooms that feel premium, load quickly, and work across modern devices.

Origin

AR has been around for nearly a decade in social platforms like Snapchat and Meta experiences, and it’s been adopted by top retailers and consumer tech brands. Prime Rig focuses on the missing piece: bringing that same interaction into practical business applications—menus, product pages, sales enablement, and real-estate visualization—without requiring an app install.
